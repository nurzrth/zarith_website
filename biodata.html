Biography

As the youngest in my family, I have always looked up to my parents, who taught me the importance of hard work and curiosity. I have a strong interest in technology and managing information, especially in health settings, which pushes me to keep learning and trying new ideas. In my free time, I like to learn about new IT tools and watch videos on YouTube, which help me stay updated and motivated. I see challenges as chances to improve and hope to make a positive difference in the world of technology. With determination and a love for learning, I believe every step I take brings me closer to achieving my goals.

Childhood Life

Growing up as the youngest in my family, I always felt a sense of warmth and support from my parents. They were my role models, teaching me the value of perseverance and curiosity from a young age. My childhood was filled with moments of learning, whether from my parents, school, or the world around me. I was a joyful child, always eager to explore new things, ask questions, and seek answers. One of the challenges I faced was learning Chinese, the third language I had to master, but with patience and support, I made progress. My siblings were also a big part of my life, and though we were different in many ways, I always admired their strengths and looked up to them for guidance. These early experiences shaped my love for discovering and understanding new ideas, especially in the world of technology.

Teenage Life

My teenage years were a time of self-discovery and growth. As I became more independent, I developed a deeper interest in subjects like science such as chemistry, biology, and physics, which sparked my curiosity about the world. I was a science stream student, and studying these subjects helped me understand the world better. Balancing school, family, and personal interests was challenging, but I learned valuable lessons in time management, responsibility, and determination. I also made lasting friendships that helped me grow socially and emotionally, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, when I realized how much I appreciated the support and connection they brought into my life. These years taught me the importance of perseverance, which has been crucial in shaping my journey.

Adult Life

Adulthood has brought its own set of challenges and opportunities for growth. Balancing the demands of university life, such as coursework, assignments, and exams, alongside personal responsibilities, has been a significant challenge. I often find myself managing my time carefully to meet deadlines while also making time for self-care and maintaining relationships with friends and family. The pressure to perform academically can be overwhelming at times, but it has taught me valuable lessons in resilience, organization, and self-discipline. Despite these challenges, I continue to be passionate about my studies in Information Management, eager to learn more and prepare for a future career in the field. However, I sometimes worry about whether I will be able to work in a field aligned with my course of study.
