Internship Experience and My Observation

Hospital Kuala Lumpur (HKL)

From 22 July 2024 to 13 September 2024, I had the privilege of undertaking an internship at Hospital Kuala Lumpur, one of Malaysia's largest and most prestigious medical institutions. As an Information Management student, this internship provided me with a platform to bridge the gap between academic learning and real-world application in a professional healthcare environment. Positioned within the hospital’s Information Technology Department, my role encompassed a wide range of responsibilities, offering me a comprehensive view of IT operations in a healthcare setting. One of my primary tasks was the installation of agents for the Sistem Pengurusan Aset dan ICT (SPAI) on government PC assets across various departments, a critical process for asset tracking and data security. This included verifying PC hostnames and ensuring each asset was correctly registered in the system. My work took me to numerous divisions, including the Human Resources Division, Radiotherapy and Nuclear Departments, and several units under the Medical and Surgical Directorates, where I collaborated with staff members and fellow interns to meet organizational objectives.

In addition to these technical tasks, I was entrusted with administrative duties, such as managing documentation at the IT Department counter, assisting staff with hardware and printer requisitions, and stamping and signing official documents. There were moments when I stepped in to answer calls and resolve inquiries in the absence of senior staff, which enhanced my confidence and communication skills. Throughout this period, I encountered challenges that tested my adaptability and problem-solving abilities, such as addressing compatibility issues during agent installations and adhering to tight schedules in busy hospital departments. I also had the opportunity to get assisted by Mrs. Suhana, my senior staff, with specific tasks like reviewing documents and providing feedback on proposal evaluations, which exposed me to administrative and evaluative processes. Encik Arif, my supervisor, and Ms. Afifah, another colleague, also helped me in several ways when I was stuck on some of the work that had been given to me. They patiently taught me how to do all of the work.

One of the most rewarding aspects of my internship was the chance to work with a team of dedicated professionals, including experienced staff members and my fellow interns, Syahiqah and Mustaqim. This collaborative environment not only allowed me to learn from their expertise but also taught me the value of teamwork and effective communication in achieving shared goals. There is one of the work where each of us have to create a poster regarding the launching of HKL navigation portal. (Click Here) to view the poster that have been created by me. Moreover, a particularly memorable moment was the intern appreciation ceremony held on our final day, where all interns were recognized for their contributions and presented with certificates of completion. This gesture of appreciation underscored the hospital’s commitment to nurturing young talent and made me feel valued for my efforts.

This internship has been a transformative experience, deepening my understanding of the integration of IT in healthcare and strengthening my technical, administrative, and interpersonal skills. It has also solidified my interest in health information management and inspired me to pursue further growth in this field. The knowledge and experiences I gained during these eight weeks have not only prepared me for future professional challenges but also instilled in me a sense of pride and purpose in contributing to the betterment of healthcare services. I am immensely grateful for this opportunity and consider it a significant milestone in my journey toward a fulfilling career in information management. Overall, it is good internship placement who want to seek out a new things.

Internship Students Celebration

The Celebration at Hospital Kuala Lumpur was a heartwarming event designed to appreciate the contributions of the interns during their time at the institution. Held on the final day of our internship, the celebration brought together the Information Technology Department staff and the interns in a casual yet meaningful gathering. The room was filled with a sense of joy as we reminisced about the journey we had shared, reflecting on both the challenges we had faced and the accomplishments we had achieved. The event remind me the importance of collaboration and the impact each individual could make when working towards a common goal.

The highlight of the celebration was the heartfelt speeches delivered by our supervisors and colleagues, who expressed their gratitude for our efforts and the enthusiasm we brought to our tasks. Their kind words were both encouraging and motivating, as they highlighted the skills and qualities we had demonstrated during our internship. As interns, we also had the opportunity to share our experiences, expressing our gratitude for the guidance and support we received throughout our journey. Although I initially felt a little shy about making a communication in front of the whole crowd, I was able to overcome it. The exchange of stories and reflections created a warm and uplifting atmosphere, further strengthening the bonds between us and the team members.

To conclude the event, we enjoyed a simple yet delightful meal together, symbolizing the unity and teamwork that had defined our time at the hospital. The celebration was a perfect way to end our internship, leaving us with lasting memories and a sense of fulfillment. It not only marked the conclusion of a significant chapter in our professional development but also inspired us to carry forward the lessons we had learned into our future endeavors. The Internship Students Celebration was more than just an event, it was a testament to the value of mutual respect, hard work, and the lasting relationships forged during our time at Hospital Kuala Lumpur.

Lastday of Internship

The last day of an internship is a bittersweet milestone, marking the end of an enriching chapter and the beginning of new opportunities. It is a moment where I can reflect on the skills that I have learned, the relationships built, and the challenges overcome during the internship journey. At Hospital Kuala Lumpur, my last day was filled with mixed emotions as I completed my final tasks and prepared to bid farewell to a workplace that had become a significant part of my life. The day began with a sense of fulfillment as I wrapped up loose ends, ensured a smooth handover of responsibilities, and expressed my gratitude to the team that guided me throughout my internship.

One of the highlights of the day was sharing heartfelt goodbyes with the colleagues and mentors who had supported me during this transformative period. Their advice and encouragement had been invaluable, and I took the opportunity to personally thank them for their patience, guidance, and trust. My fellow interns and I reminisced about the memorable moments we shared, from navigating challenging tasks to celebrating small victories together. It was also a time to exchange contact information, ensuring we stayed connected as we pursued our respective career paths. The camaraderie built during the internship made the farewell particularly poignant, as it underscored the depth of the bonds we had formed. Both supervisors above also were especially kind and supportive to the interns, always taking the time to explain tasks clearly, offer helpful feedback, and make sure we felt included in the team. Their kindness created a welcoming and positive atmosphere, which made learning and growing easier.

As the day drew to a close, I left the hospital with a profound sense of gratitude and accomplishment. The internship had been a stepping stone in my professional journey, equipping me with practical skills and experiences that would shape my future. It also instilled in me a deeper understanding of the importance of teamwork, resilience, and adaptability in a real-world setting. While it was difficult to say goodbye, the lessons and memories from my time at Hospital Kuala Lumpur remain a cherished part of my life, inspiring me to approach future endeavors with confidence and determination. I hope to experience the hospital work environment again during my future degree internship.
